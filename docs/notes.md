# Presentation Notes

## Slide 1. Title Slide

Open by framing the talk clearly. This is not a speculative presentation about hype or futuristic transhumanism. It is a governance presentation about AI-enabled augmentation systems and the institutional framework needed before such systems become normalised.

## Slide 2. Why Governance Must Change Now

Emphasise three changes at once: continuity, adaptivity, and infrastructural embedding. Older interventions were episodic and clinically bounded. AI-enabled enhancement is adaptive over time, connected to platforms, and partly governed outside the clinic.

## Slide 3. Fragmented Rules, Connected Systems

Be precise here: the European Union already regulates this space in important ways. The point is not absence of regulation, but fragmentation. Existing rules are strong yet sectoral, and they do not fully govern the whole feedback loop or long-term dependency.

## Slide 4. What Counts as AI-Enabled Human Enhancement?

Introduce the paper's working definition. Not every AI tool in medicine is enhancement. The category requires three features together: direct impact on bodily or cognitive function, adaptive optimisation, and ongoing technical or economic dependency.

## Slide 5. The Fundamental Rights Baseline

Use this as the normative anchor. Fundamental rights are not abstract ethical decoration here. They become concrete design constraints for systems that intervene in the body, cognition, autonomy, and data environment of the individual.

## Slide 6. The Threat Map

This slide should establish the full problem space. The key point is that these are not isolated failures. They are recurring structural mechanisms: coercion, misuse, control, and dependency.

## Slide 7. From Therapy to Adversarial Use

On militarisation, stress that hierarchy weakens voluntariness even without explicit force. On biological misuse, emphasise that AI lowers the cost and increases the speed of design, optimisation, and potentially targeted harm.

## Slide 8. Connected Implants, Cyber-Physical Risk

This should be delivered strongly. A useful example is an implant or insulin pump: once a device communicates with an external system, cybersecurity is no longer just a technical issue. It becomes a direct bodily integrity and safety issue.

## Slide 9. From Care Data to Social Sorting

Clarify that the central problem is not only leakage. The deeper problem is inference and profiling. Health, biometric, and genetic data can be transformed into selection, scoring, and exclusion in work, insurance, and finance.

## Slide 10. When Choice Is Shaped by Pressure

Connect both halves of the slide with one theme: formally voluntary choice can still be structurally shaped by pressure. In one case the mechanism is economic lock-in and pay-to-live dependency; in the other it is competitive pressure to upgrade.

## Slide 11. The Codex of Augmented Humanity

This is the conceptual centre of the talk. Explain that the Codex is not an ideological manifesto. It is a governance layer that translates fundamental rights into actionable red lines and constraints.

## Slide 12. Turning Principles into Controls

This is where you show implementation credibility. Stress that governance is not only about principles. It must cover the whole lifecycle: design, runtime, maintenance, and institutional oversight.

## Slide 13. Rights-Based Governance Enables Benefit

This slide balances the talk. State clearly that the paper is not anti-innovation. Proper governance enables socially legitimate and therapeutically valuable applications by preventing exploitative or coercive uses.

## Slide 14. What This Paper Contributes

Deliver this with the three verbs: define, map, operationalise. That makes the contribution easy for the audience to remember and gives a clean summary of the paper's scientific value.

## Slide 15. Closing Slide

Close firmly but without catastrophism. The final claim is simple: the framework must be built now, before technical, economic, and institutional dependencies become normal infrastructure for augmentation.
